Education in America

Introduction

This page lists the four core subjects of American education and their definitions.

"Learning is not attained by chance. It must be sought for with ardour and attended with diligence.” - Abigail Adams
Language Arts

noun: language arts; plural noun: language artses

1. the study of grammar, composition, spelling, and (sometimes) public speaking, typically taught as a single subject in elementary and middle school.

  • Reading
  • Writing
"Knowledge of languages is the doorway to wisdom." - Roger Bacon
Mathematics

noun

1. the abstract science of number, quantity, and space. Mathematics may be studied in its own right ( pure mathematics ), or as it is applied to other disciplines such as physics and engineering ( applied mathematics ).

  • Addition
  • Subtraction
  • Multiplication
  • Division
"Pure mathematics is, in its way, the poetry of logical ideas." - Albert Einstein
Science

noun

1. the systematic study of the structure and behavior of the physical and natural world through observation, experimentation, and the testing of theories against the evidence obtained.

2. knowledge of any kind.

  • Physics
  • Chemistry
  • Biology
“Nothing has such power to broaden the mind as the ability to investigate systematically and truly all that comes under thy observation in life.” – Marcus Aurelius
History

noun

1. the study of past events, particularly in human affairs.

2. the whole series of past events connected with someone or something.

  • Social
  • Political
  • Economic
  • Cultural
"We are not makers of history. We are made by history." - Martin Luther King, Jr.
